      • CSS: Style sheet language for describing the presentation of a document.

      • JavaScript: Widely used for client-side scripting to enable interactive web pages.

    • History

    The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) was founded in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web, to develop protocols and guidelines to ensure the long-term growth of the web. Here’s a brief history: 1. **1994**: Tim Berners-Lee establishes W3C at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) to promote web standards and interoperability. 2. **1995-2000**: The consortium releases foundational technologies, including HTML, CSS, and the XML family of standards, fostering the growth of web content and design. 3. **2001**: The W3C develops the Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI) to promote web accessibility for people with disabilities. 4. **2004**: The first version of the Web Services Architecture is published, promoting interoperability between web services. 5. **2008**: The HTML5 specification begins development, aiming to enhance the capabilities of HTML for modern web applications. 6. **2014**: The W3C and WHATWG (Web Hypertext Application Technology Working Group) begin collaborating on the HTML standard, leading to a more open and community-driven approach. 7. **2019**: The W3C continues to address issues like privacy, security, and decentralization while advancing technologies like WebAssembly and the Semantic Web. The W3C has played a crucial role in shaping the web into a universal platform for communication, commerce, and creativity, with an ongoing commitment to openness and accessibility.
